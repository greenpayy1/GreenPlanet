Green Planet Eco Certification

A universal mark of trust for buildings, hotels, offices, cafés, restaurants, schools, universities, kindergartens, beauty salons, and electrical cars.

Green Planet Eco Certified Global Standards

Our certification evaluates eight domains across energy, water, buildings, waste, food, transport, social impact, and governance. Below is a concise overview of the requirements we assess.

1. Energy & Climate

  • Use of renewable energy sources (solar panels, wind energy, green tariffs).
  • Minimization of CO₂ and greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Carbon footprint accounting and a reduction plan.

2. Water & Resources

  • Water-saving systems (aerators, reuse, rainwater harvesting).
  • Minimization of leaks and water losses.
  • Eco-friendly wastewater treatment technologies.

3. Buildings & Infrastructure

  • Use of energy-efficient and eco-friendly building materials.
  • Certification under international standards (LEED, BREEAM, Estidama, etc.).
  • Availability of green zones: vertical gardens, hydroponic walls, landscaping.

4. Waste Management

  • Separate collection and recycling of waste.
  • Reduction of single-use plastics.
  • Programs for reuse of materials.

5. Food & Products

  • Use of organic, local, and hydroponic products.
  • Reduction of food waste (food-sharing, composting).
  • Minimization of packaging; use of eco-friendly containers.

6. Transport & Mobility

  • Use of electric or hybrid vehicles.
  • Installation of EV charging stations.
  • Plans to encourage public transport and cycling.

7. Social Responsibility

  • Employee support programs (health, eco‑habit training).
  • Local community involvement in eco‑projects.
  • Fair procurement and partnership policies.

8. Management & Reporting

  • Presence of a documented environmental policy.
  • Annual sustainability report on achievements.
  • Transparency and accessibility of data for auditors and the public.

Minimum Conditions for Certification

  • A documented environmental policy.
  • Compliance with at least 70% of criteria in each section.
  • Implementation of a monitoring and auditing system.
  • Verification by an independent expert (auditor).
